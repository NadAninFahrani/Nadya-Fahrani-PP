What distinguishes us from each other are brain chemicals

Serotonin

(happiness and contentedness)

Serotonin is produced in the Raphe Nuclei

Serotoninergic system

Raphe Nuclei:

Cell bodies of serotonin-producing neurons are clustered in groups.

Ascending Serotonin Pathways:

Regulation of emotional processing and other functions.

Descending Serotonin Pathways:

Involved in modulating pain and spinal reflexes.

Distribution:

Some are involved in mood regulation, while others affect nausea and appetite.

Serotonin goes hand in hand with eachother

The initial base of dopamine is how you're normally feeling throughout the day but because we seek dopamine constantly, when we go back to our normal mood, we mistake it as sadness

Because we seek constant dopamine, the peak of dopamine won't feel as good because we don't have enough time to “refill” our dopamine levels

Dopamine

(excitement and motivation)

Dopamine is produced in the substantia nigra, ventral tegmental area, and hypothalamus.

4 Major Dopamine Pathways

Mesolimbic Pathway:

Brain's reward system and the experience of pleasure and motivation.

Mesocortical Pathway:

Cognitive functions, executive control, and emotional regulation.

Nigrostriatal Pathway:

Motor control and coordination.

Tuberoinfundibular Pathway:

Regulates the release of prolactin.

Social Media and Focusing

Both serotonin and dopamine play roles in attention and focus. Imbalances in these neurotransmitters can affect attention span and concentration.

Many people claim that social media is reducing individuals' attention span. Rather, pyschologist claim that it reduces our retention span.

Retention Span

The ability to retain and recall information over a period of time.

Attention Span

Attention span refers to the amount of time an individual can focus on a particular task or activity without becoming distracted.

Addictions

Addiction is a complex condition characterized by the compulsive engagement in rewarding stimuli despite adverse consequences. Addiction is associated with changes in the brain's reward system, leading to a strong craving for the substance or behavior and a diminished ability to control the impulse to engage in it.

SOCIAL MEDIA AND DRUG ADDICTION WILL SOON BE IN THE SAME CATEGORY BECAUSE OF SIMILAR STIMULIS

SOCIAL MEDIA ADDICTION

Factors contributing to social media addiction include the constant availability of social media, the instant gratification it provides, and the reinforcement of social connections.

DRUG ADDICTION

It is characterized by a pattern of continued use despite negative consequences, tolerance (needing increasing amounts for the same effect), and withdrawal symptoms when not using the substance.

INFORMATION OVERLOAD

Information overload refers to the situation in which an individual is exposed to an excessive amount of information that exceeds their capacity to process and absorb it effectively.

When scrolling through social media, all the information, whether unimportant or not, gets processed in your brain leading to burnout over a period of time.

Similar to exams week, when everyone is cramming information to their heads, they'll often feel nauseous, dizzy, and fatigued. Your brain has a capacity, if you keep on overloading it with information, it's soon going to get tired.

SIGMUND FREUD

Sigmund Freud was an Austrian neurologist and the founder of psychoanalysis, a clinical method for treating psychopathology through dialogue between a patient and a psychoanalyst. Freud developed a structural model of the mind that consists of three components: the ID, the Ego, and the Superego.

ID (pleasure)

It operates on the pleasure principle, seeking immediate gratification of basic needs and desires without concern for consequences.

EGO (decision)

The Ego operates on the reality principle, balancing the demands of the ID with the constraints of the external world.

SUPEREGO (morals)

The Superego strives for perfection and imposes a sense of guilt or shame when an individual deviates from moral or social standards.
